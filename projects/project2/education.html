Education

Being in person without restrictions can be crucial to child development and teaching. We have seen different effects in the country between elementary schools, middle/high schools, and colleges.

Elementary Schools

Younger students have probably been hurt the most from learning remotely. So much of learning as a child is working hands-on with different activities and new ideas. While a positive may be younger kids are learning how to use technology, the cons outweigh the pros by a long shot. Some of the biggest issues include an increased screen time for children, classes having to teach at a slower pace, and parents having to basically go to school with their kids. Luckily, younger students were some of the first to get back in the classroom, but a lot of damage was done in the time they were essentially homeschooled.

Middle and High Schools

Older students doing school online caused a lot of issues, though it was mainly for teachers. Because these students change classes at least once every hour of school, there were many connectivity issues, and more problems with getting class started with everyone there and listening than elementary schools. Another major issue was keeping students engaged. Middle and high school students tend to get distracted and bored much easier, and a lot of them did not pay attention in their online courses. In addition, a lot of students reported having gaming consoles in their rooms, which was a huge distraction, causing a lot of students to be playing games instead of listening to class.

College Students

College students also got greatly impacted by the pandemic. Though because college is something most people have to pay a lot of money for, students reported being more engaged in online classes. The biggest issue was teamwork. A lot of classes in college required students to work collaboratively, which is a major challenge when working online. Accountability can drop, work ethic decreases, and communication is a major challenge. A lot of schools had to adjust curriculum completely for their classes, causing students to be slightly thrown off on their paths to graduation and a job in the workforce. Along with this, classes also had to be taught at slower paces, causing a lot more students to fall behind and eventually fail the course they were taking.

In addition to all of the issues talked about above, we also run into problems with internet connection, and paying attention. Having to rely on an internet connection that is not consistent for everyone can cause issues with pacing in the classroom and deadlines for workers. Also, it is extremely easy to lose focus when at home, and it has been brutal for everyone involved.
